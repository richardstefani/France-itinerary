**Evening:**

**Notes:**

### DAY 12 – MONDAY, 13 JULY: LAVENDER FIELDS & PROVENÇAL COUNTRYSIDE (BASTILLE DAY EVE)

**Theme:** Iconic Provençal purple fields, regional landscape, sensory experience, Bastille Day atmosphere

**Note:** Mid-July lavender is past peak bloom (peak is late June–early July), but fields still beautiful & fragrant. If appearance/density matters most, this is realistic expectation. Landscape itself is stunning.

**Morning:**

  * **7:45 AM** – Breakfast in Aix
  * **8:45 AM** – Car hire pickup (or pick up from Day 14 rental)
    * If not already rented, arrange car for this day + Days 14–17
  * **9:00 AM–1:00 PM** – Drive to **Lavender Country** (Provence Lavande Region)
    * **Route:** Aix → Valensole Plateau (~1 hour drive northeast via D8)
    * **Valensole** is heart of lavender country – iconic purple fields cover landscape in summer
    * Mid-July: fields past absolute peak (June peak), but still aromatic & visually impressive
    * Stop at scenic overlooks, take photographs
    * Walk through fields if accessible (check property access – some are private)
    * Visit local producer for lavender products (soap, honey, essential oils)

### **French Phrases & Reservations**

**Booking a Restaurant:**

  * “Je voudrais réserver une table pour deux, le [date] à [time]” (I’d like to reserve a table for two on [date] at [time])
  * “Réservé au nom de [Name]” (Under the name…)
  * Book online via TheFork.fr or call directly

**Wine Tasting Etiquette:**

  * Tastings are social; ask winemaker questions
  * Spitting (into buckets provided) is acceptable & common
  * Purchasing a bottle after tasting is polite but not obligatory

**Basic French:**

  * “Bonjour” (Good morning/day)
  * “S’il vous plaît” (Please – formal)
  * “Merci” (Thank you)
  * “L’addition, s’il vous plaît” (The bill, please)
