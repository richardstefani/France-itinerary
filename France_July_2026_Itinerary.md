### **DAY 1 – THURSDAY, 2 JULY: ARRIVAL & SETTLING IN**

**Theme:** Arrive, decompress, first taste of Antibes

**Morning:**
- **10:00 AM** – Arrive Nice Côte d'Azur Airport
- **10:30–11:15 AM** – Travel to Antibes
  - *Recommended:* Train from Nice to Antibes (~30 min direct, €12–15 per person) — quickest option, no parking hassle
  - *Alternative:* Rental car (~45 min via A8 motorway) — useful if you plan to keep a car for the week
- **12:00 PM** – Check in to accommodation (aim for Old Town or harbour-adjacent location)
- **12:30–1:30 PM** – Lunch near harbour — first meal, keep it casual
  - *Suggested:* **La Boîte à Matelots** (relaxed, family-friendly, right on the port) or **Le Vauban** (waterfront terrace)

**Afternoon:**
- **2:00–5:00 PM** – Explore Antibes Old Town at leisure
  - Wander the narrow Provençal streets; spot Château Grimaldi (houses the Picasso Museum)
  - Walk the ramparts for views over the Med and the Baie des Anges
  - Browse the covered Marché Provençal on Cours Masséna (open daily in summer)
- **5:30 PM** – First swim at **Plage de la Gravette** (sheltered cove below the ramparts, calm water, ideal for first-day dip) or **Plage de Ponteil**

**Evening:**
- **7:30 PM** – Aperitif then dinner — easy, celebratory first night
  - *Suggested:* **Gaston & Gastronome** (relaxed bistro, good for families) or **Le Comptoir** (buzzy, central)

**Overnight:** Antibes

### **DAY 2 – FRIDAY, 3 JULY: CAP D'ANTIBES WALK + WATERSPORTS**

**Theme:** Get active — coastal scenery in the morning, adrenaline on the water in the afternoon

**Morning:**
- **8:30 AM** – Breakfast at a local café (Rue Sade area or Cours Masséna)
- **9:30 AM–12:30 PM** – **Sentier du Littoral, Cap d'Antibes coastal walk**
  - One of the best walks on the entire Riviera — dramatic limestone cliffs, hidden coves, crystal clear water
  - ~2 hours at a relaxed pace; bring swimwear as there are multiple spots to jump in
  - Start at Plage de la Garoupe and follow the coastal path south around the Cap
  - Views back to Antibes Old Town and out to the Îles de Lérins
  - *Note: In summer 2025 the white segment near Baie des Milliardaires was partially closed for renovation — check current status before going*

**Lunch:**
- **1:00–2:00 PM** – Lunch back in Antibes Old Town (post-walk reward)
  - *Suggested:* **Tête de Lard** (casual, cheerful) or **L'Arome** (Provençal, good value)

**Afternoon — Watersports at Juan-les-Pins:**
- **3:00–6:00 PM** – Head to Juan-les-Pins beachfront (~15 min walk or short bus from Antibes)
  - **Snorkelling with Seabob** at La Garoupe Bay — underwater scooters are a big hit with teenagers; guided sessions available
  - **Stand-up paddleboard hire** from Cape Kayak or the sailing club at Salis Beach (€15–20/hr)
  - Or simply swim and explore the rocky coastline by the Cap

**Evening:**
- **7:30 PM** – Dinner in Antibes
  - *Suggested:* **Pizzeria La Mamma** (fun, relaxed, perfect after an active day) or **Les Vins du Coin** (wine bar with sharing plates)

**Overnight:** Antibes

### **DAY 3 – SATURDAY, 4 JULY: NICE DAY TRIP**

**Theme:** Cosmopolitan Riviera — urban energy, markets, beach

**Morning:**
- **8:30 AM** – Train from Antibes to Nice (~25 min, €12–15 per person)
- **9:15–11:00 AM** – **Vieux Nice** exploration
  - Colourful Baroque architecture, narrow alleyways, Cours Saleya flower and food market (Saturday is especially lively)
  - Street food: socca (chickpea pancake), pan bagnat — ideal for a walking breakfast/snack
  - Boys will enjoy the urban buzz and market atmosphere
- **11:00 AM–12:30 PM** – Walk up to **Colline du Château** (Castle Hill)
  - Free entry; panoramic views over the Promenade, old port and Baie des Anges
  - Take the lift or climb the steps; good photo spot

**Lunch:**
- **1:00–2:30 PM** – Lunch in Vieux Nice
  - *Suggested:* **Salière** (proper Niçoise cuisine, good value), **La Maison de Marie** (rustic, reliable), or grab food from the market stalls on Cours Saleya

**Afternoon:**
- **2:30–5:30 PM** – **Promenade des Anglais** & beach
  - Walk the famous promenade; the boys can rent bikes along the seafront
  - Swim at Plage de Nice (pebbly but the water is gorgeous in July)
  - Ice cream stop — look for Fenocchio in Old Town (legendary, 100+ flavours)

**Evening:**
- **6:00 PM** – Train back to Antibes
- **7:30 PM** – Dinner in Antibes
  - *Suggested:* **Café Poulailler** (lively, relaxed) or **Le Bacon** (upscale seafood — worth treating yourselves)

**Overnight:** Antibes

### **DAY 4 – SUNDAY, 5 JULY: CANNES + ÎLES DE LÉRINS BOAT TRIP**

**Theme:** The full Riviera experience — glamour, islands, snorkelling

**Morning:**
- **8:30 AM** – Train from Antibes to Cannes (~15 min, €5–7)
- **9:00–10:30 AM** – Quick explore of **Cannes**
  - Walk the Croisette Boulevard; photo stop at the Palais des Festivals (red carpet steps)
  - Wander Vieux Cannes (Le Suquet hill) for views and local atmosphere

**Mid-Morning — Îles de Lérins boat trip (highlight of the week):**
- **10:30 AM** – Board ferry or private boat from Cannes port to **Îles de Lérins**
  - Ferries run regularly from Cannes port; ~15 min crossing
  - *Recommended:* Book a private half-day boat charter from Antibes or Cannes for a family of 4 — skipper takes you to the best swim and snorkel spots between the islands (~€200–300 for a half-day split four ways)
  - **Île Sainte-Marguerite** — hidden coves, crystal-clear water, snorkelling, walking trails; no cars on the island
  - **Île Saint-Honorat** — 1,600-year-old Cistercian monastery; monks produce award-winning organic wine (gift shop open to visitors)
  - Snack food boats come to the beaches in summer — you can order pizza and sandwiches and wine from the water

**Lunch:**
- **1:00–2:00 PM** – Picnic on the island or eat at the island café; or back in Cannes
  - *Suggested if back in Cannes:* **Le Squaw** (casual, sea views) or **Gastromimétis** (upscale casual)

**Afternoon:**
- **3:00–5:30 PM** – Beach time in Cannes (Plage du Midi) or head back to Antibes for a swim at Gravette

**Evening:**
- **6:00–6:30 PM** – Train back to Antibes
- **7:30 PM** – Dinner
  - *Suggested:* **Chez Fred** (good seafood, casual) or **Comptoir Méditerranée** (Mediterranean plates)

**Overnight:** Antibes

### **DAY 5 – MONDAY, 6 JULY: WATERSPORTS DAY — JET SKIS, WAKEBOARD & PARASAILING**

**Theme:** Maximum adrenaline — the teenage highlight of the week

**Morning:**
- **9:00 AM** – Leisurely breakfast (you'll want energy for today)
- **10:00 AM** – Head to Juan-les-Pins beachfront (~15 min walk or short bus)

**Mid-Morning–Afternoon — Watersports session:**

  - Full menu: jet skis, waterskiing, wakeboard, wakesurf, parasailing, canoe, paddleboard
  - They adapt to all levels — perfect for a mixed-ability family
  - *Suggested combination:* Jet ski tour + wakeboard session + parasailing for the boys
  - Budget: €50–120 per person depending on activities chosen; book ahead in July
- **Alternative / add-on:** **Efoiling at Cap d'Antibes** — electric hydrofoil surfing; genuinely futuristic and a huge hit with teenagers; introductory sessions available for beginners

**Lunch:**
- **1:30–2:30 PM** – Lunch in Juan-les-Pins (beachside)
  - *Suggested:* Beachside café or restaurant on the Promenade Amiral Courbet

**Afternoon:**
- **3:00–6:00 PM** – Free beach time; swim, recover, people-watch
  - Juan-les-Pins has a livelier beach scene than Antibes Old Town — good music, beach bars, summer atmosphere

**Evening:**
- **7:30 PM** – Dinner back in Antibes
  - *Suggested:* **Le Safari** (upscale casual, treat after a big day) or **Mickaël Féval** (Michelin-starred seafood — book well ahead, worth it)

### **DAY 6 – TUESDAY, 7 JULY: ANTIBES RELAXED DAY — PICASSO, PORT VAUBAN & BEACH**

**Theme:** Slow down, explore what's on the doorstep, recharge after a big week

**Morning:**
- **8:30 AM** – Leisurely breakfast at a café on Cours Masséna
- **10:00 AM–12:00 PM** – **Musée Picasso (Château Grimaldi)**
  - Housed in a 12th-century château right on the ramparts above the sea
  - Picasso spent the summer of 1946 here and donated 23 paintings and over 70 drawings to the town — the collection is genuinely special
  - Entry ~€8 per person; the terrace alone with its sea views is worth the visit
  - *If the boys aren't art fans:* skip inside and just walk the ramparts and cliff-top gardens around the château for the views

**Late Morning:**
- **12:00–1:00 PM** – Wander **Port Vauban** — the largest marina in Europe
  - The Billionaires' Quay is lined with extraordinary superyachts; worth a slow stroll
  - Good photo spot and free entertainment

**Lunch:**
- **1:00–2:30 PM** – Relaxed lunch in Old Town
  - *Suggested:* **Tête de Lard** (casual, fun atmosphere) or **L'Arome** (Provençal, good value)

**Afternoon:**
- **3:00–6:00 PM** – Extended beach time
  - **Plage de la Garoupe** on Cap d'Antibes is worth the 20-min bus ride — wider, less crowded than the town beaches, good snorkelling off the rocks at the eastern end
  - Or stay local at **Plage de la Gravette** for a lazy final full afternoon swim

**Evening:**
- **7:00 PM** – Aperitif at a harbour terrace; watch the boats
- **8:00 PM** – Dinner
  - *Suggested:* **Le Safari** (upscale casual, good for a group) or **Café Poulailler** (lively, relaxed)

**Overnight:** Antibes

### **DAY 13 – TUESDAY, 14 JULY: BASTILLE DAY**

**Theme:** France's national day — military parade, market, Provençal picnic & fireworks at the Rotonde

**Morning:**
- **9:00 AM** – Watch the **military parade** through Aix (typically through the town centre; check locally for exact route and timing)
- **10:00 AM** – Coffee and croissant at a Cours Mirabeau terrace; soak up the national day atmosphere

**Late Morning — Picnic Supplies:**
- **10:30 AM–12:30 PM** – Gather picnic supplies from the market and local shops
  - **Place Richelme market** (open daily, morning): bread, cheese, charcuterie, fruit
  - A good cave (wine shop) on or near Cours Mirabeau for a bottle of Provence rosé or two
  - *Suggested shops:* **La Cave du Félibrige** or any fromagerie/charcuterie in the Old Town
  - This is the French way to do Bastille Day — don't overthink it, just buy what looks good

**Lunch:**
- **12:30–2:00 PM** – Relax at the accommodation or find a shaded square; enjoy your market haul

**Afternoon:**
- **2:30–5:30 PM** – Unstructured time
  - Nap, wander, browse the streets; the town has a festive, unhurried energy today
  - Note: most museums will be closed; tourist shops may be open morning only

**Early Evening — Set Up for the Night:**
- **6:00 PM** – Head to the **Rotonde fountain area** to find a good spot
  - The Cours Mirabeau and Rotonde fill up steadily from early evening; earlier is better for a comfortable pitch
  - Bring a blanket, your remaining picnic supplies, and whatever wine is left
- **7:00–9:30 PM** – The **Cours Mirabeau** fills with music and dance performances from local artists; the *bal populaire* (communal street dancing) gets going — join in or watch from a café terrace
- **9:30 PM** – Top up drinks; settle in near the Rotonde for the best fireworks view

**Evening — Fireworks:**
- **~10:00–11:00 PM** – **Fireworks display over the Rotonde fountain** — the centrepiece of Aix's Bastille Day
  - Free entry; arrive well before 10pm for a good spot
  - *Note:* In extreme heat, Provence fireworks can occasionally be cancelled as a fire precaution — worth a quick check with the local tourist office on the day
- **Post-fireworks** – Roads and squares will be busy for 30–45 min after; no rush, let the crowds thin over a last drink at a nearby bar

**Overnight:** Aix-en-Provence
